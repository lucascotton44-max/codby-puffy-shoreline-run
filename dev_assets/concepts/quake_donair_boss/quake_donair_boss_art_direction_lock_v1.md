# Quake Donair Boss Art Direction Lock v1

## 1. Purpose

This document locks the art direction target for the future Quake Donair Boss source sheet before any more image generation.

It exists to prevent drift toward photoreal portrait work, generic human model sheets, mascot parody, or cluttered concept posters. The next generated boss sheet should be useful for runtime sprite production, not just a funny standalone image.

This is an art-direction document only. It does not approve code, runtime assets, public use, or final implementation.

## 2. Why Previous Attempts Are Not Final

The current Quake boss attempts are not final source art for these reasons:

- Too realistic: the boss reads too close to a real person or photo-based character instead of a Calvin sketch-world game enemy.
- Too human-model-sheet: the poses feel like a standard character design lineup rather than a playable side-scroller boss translated through Calvin's rough drawing language.
- Not Calvin sketch-world enough: the silhouette, linework, and texture need to live closer to the Red Bart, Earth Eyes Bart, and Melted Characters source references.
- Too much likeness pressure: the design must avoid exact likeness unless permission is clean and explicit. Even with permission, the runtime design should be a respectful sketch-world guest boss, not a portrait.
- Title/window/background clutter: giant title text, store windows, signage, scenery, and poster elements make the boss sheet harder to use for runtime sprites.

The Old Variety arena source concept is the environment reference, not a reason to bake windows, signs, or background props into the boss source sheet.

## 3. Final Boss Design Target

The final source target is:

- Sketch-world guest boss.
- Chunky readable silhouette for side-scroller gameplay.
- Simplified face with expressive attitude, not an exact portrait.
- Dark hoodie or streetwear shape language.
- Cap/hood influence, simplified into a bold playable silhouette.
- Local legend energy without relying on likeness precision.
- Respectful, not parody.
- Funny mechanic, grounded tone: the donair throw is the joke, but the character is treated with care.

The boss should feel like someone remembered through Calvin's sketch-world: a larger-than-life local figure pulled into a strange playable memory, not a celebrity caricature.

## 4. Required Gameplay Poses

The next source sheet must include exactly these gameplay-useful poses:

1. Idle facing left.
2. Wind-up holding donair.
3. Throw/release left.
4. Stunned counter-window.
5. Humbled defeat, no injury.

Pose requirements:

- Full body visible in every pose.
- Clear left-facing side-scroller readability.
- Consistent proportions across poses.
- No labels baked into the character art.
- No background, arena, window, or sign props in the boss pose sheet.

## 5. Hard Rejects

Reject any generation that includes:

- Photoreal portrait.
- Exact likeness.
- Gore, blood, injury, bruising, or humiliation.
- Giant title text.
- Window or background prop baked into the character sheet.
- Real logos.
- Generic rapper caricature.
- Mascot treatment.
- Demon or fantasy boss treatment.
- Neon portal effects.
- Public-facing endorsement cues.
- Storefront signage attached to the character.
- Any design that cannot be converted into clean runtime sprites.

## 6. Image Generation Prompt v2

Create a clean production source sheet for a side-scroller bonus boss called Quake - Donair Toss Boss.

Design a respectful fictionalized sketch-world guest boss inspired by local legend energy, not an exact likeness. The character should have a chunky readable side-scroller silhouette, simplified expressive face, dark hoodie or streetwear shape language, cap or hood influence, confident stance, and a grounded funny tone. The boss throws donairs as the mechanic, but the character should not be a joke mascot.

Style: Calvin sketch-world translated into game-ready 2D art. Rough handmade black linework, marker texture, uneven sketch energy, simplified shapes, readable at small platformer scale. Match the spirit of the Red Bart, Earth Eyes Bart, and Melted Characters source sheets: strange, handmade, expressive, and playable. Respectful local legend energy, not parody.

Sheet layout: one clean source sheet with five full-body poses of the same boss, facing left where applicable:
1. idle facing left
2. wind-up holding donair
3. throw/release left
4. stunned counter-window
5. humbled defeat, no injury

The boss wears dark hoodie/streetwear with cap or hood influence. The donair prop should be readable but simple. Use a neutral light background or transparent background. No arena, no storefront, no window, no scenery, no giant title, no labels, no real logos.

Tone: funny mechanic, grounded memory-store mood, respectful, sketch-world weirdness, Atlantic local flavor, not fantasy.

Output: high-resolution source sheet suitable for later runtime sprite extraction.

## 7. Negative Prompt v2

Do not create a photoreal portrait. Do not create an exact likeness. Do not create a generic rapper caricature. Do not make the boss a mascot, demon, monster, superhero, anime character, fantasy villain, or polished cartoon celebrity.

No gore, blood, injury, bruising, weapons, humiliation, or mean parody. No giant title text, no labels, no letters baked into the character, no real logos, no storefront window, no background props, no scenery, no crowd, no brand signage, no public endorsement cues.

Do not make it too realistic, too human-model-sheet, too slick, too neon, too fantasy, or too cluttered. Do not include the Old Variety arena as the background. Do not make the donair projectile the main character.

## 8. Acceptance Checklist

Accept the next boss sheet only if:

- It clearly reads as a sketch-world guest boss.
- It avoids exact likeness and photoreal portrait pressure.
- The silhouette is chunky and readable at side-scroller scale.
- The face is simplified and expressive.
- Hoodie/streetwear and cap/hood influence are visible without real logos.
- The donair wind-up and throw poses are immediately understandable.
- The stunned counter-window pose is readable and fair-looking.
- The humbled defeat pose has no injury, gore, or humiliation.
- The sheet has no giant title text.
- The sheet has no windows, storefront, background props, real signage, or logos.
- The tone is respectful and funny without becoming parody.
- The design feels compatible with Calvin's existing Bart and Melt visual language.
- The source sheet can guide clean runtime sprite extraction.

Reject and regenerate if any pose looks like a realistic human portrait, a generic rapper caricature, a mascot, a demon, or a fantasy boss.

## 9. Save / Commit Rule

Only save the next generated sheet if it is strong enough to guide runtime sprites.

Do not save weak generations just because they are amusing. Do not save sheets with likeness risk, clutter, background dependency, giant text, or unusable poses.

If a generation is not production-useful, discard it and revise the prompt before trying again.

Do not create runtime PNGs until the source sheet is approved.

Do not commit this direction or any future boss source art until the working tree contains only the intended scoped files and the build passes.
